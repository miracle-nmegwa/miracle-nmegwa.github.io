Top 3 Segments by Sales ($M)

Operating Margin by Segment

  • Women's Apparel – South (Outlet) leads on margin at 53.6%, while Men's Street Footwear – Southeast (Online) leads on volume at $19.3M in sales.
  • K-Means clustering (validated by silhouette analysis) separated the portfolio into distinct segments by sales scale, margin, and unit efficiency — surfacing 10 priority segments for investment.
  • One-way ANOVA confirmed the regional and product performance differences are statistically real, not noise.
  • Holt-Winters exponential smoothing was used for seasonal demand forecasting, backtested against historical data.
  • Lifting margin by 5 percentage points on the lowest-performing segments alone is worth roughly $12.8M in additional profit.

Cyclistic Bike-Share: Casual-to-Member Conversion

Google Data Analytics Capstone — 12 months of Chicago bike-share trip data, member vs. casual behaviour.

R · Tidyverse · ggplot2 · Tableau
5.86MTotal Trips
692Stations
5,824Bikes
15–20%Target Conversion

Total Rides: Casual vs. Member

Average Ride Length (minutes)

  • Members outnumber casual riders in trip volume (3.19M vs. 2.48M), but casual riders take rides 2x longer on average (26 min vs. 13 min) — a recreational pattern rather than a commuting one.
  • Casual ridership peaks on weekends and in July; member ridership stays steady on weekdays and peaks in September — classic leisure-vs-commute signatures.
  • Both groups favour classic bikes; members essentially never use docked bikes.
  • Recommended a weekend-only membership tier, summer-targeted promotions, and station-level marketing at the top 5 casual-heavy stations — projected to convert 15–20% of casual riders.
