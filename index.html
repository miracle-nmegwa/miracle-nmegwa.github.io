Research

My current research asks a deceptively simple question: does digital adoption spread opportunity across Nigeria's regions, or does it concentrate further where capacity already exists?

Nigerian Economic Society (NES) Conference Paper — with an academic mentor
Does Digitalisation Equalise or Stratify? Digital Adoption and Regional Inequality Dynamics in Nigeria

Built a 37-state × 12-year panel dataset and ran a full spatial panel econometric analysis in R. Along the way: redefined the Digital Adoption Index around broadband penetration after a PCA collapse from near-perfect collinearity, retained only the fiscal-capacity interaction term after two others failed to survive, and worked through several data-quality corrections — misspelled state names, incorrect survey variable codes, an IGR double-counting bug, and a population-vintage discontinuity — before trusting the results.

The most robust finding so far: regional inequality in Nigeria isn't scattered — it clusters geographically and persists, which is exactly what that spatial autocorrelation coefficient is picking up.
